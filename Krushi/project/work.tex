\chapter{Experiment and Result Analysis}
\paragraph{}The Krushi system was tested by evaluating its major modules, including plant disease detection, soil analysis, chatbot assistance, and the e-commerce platform. In the experiment, various leaf images were uploaded to test the accuracy of disease detection, and different soil parameters such as pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium values were provided to check the effectiveness of crop recommendations. Additionally, multiple user queries were given to the chatbot to analyze its response quality, and the shopping features were tested by performing product selection, cart management, and order placement.
\paragraph{}The results showed that the plant disease detection module provided accurate predictions, while the soil analysis module generated suitable crop recommendations based on the input data. The chatbot responded effectively to user queries and provided helpful agricultural guidance. The e-commerce module functioned smoothly, allowing users to browse products and complete transactions without issues. Overall, the system performed efficiently and proved to be useful in assisting farmers, improving decision-making, and supporting better agricultural productivity.
